    Future Goals

    - Parse a chat log between at least 2 people and determine which user is the most toxic

    - Log any series of phrases and categorize them based on their sentiment analysis -- beyond toxic or non-toxic, depending on whatever the person forking our code wants to do with our NLP algorithms

    Lessons Learned

    - Data cleaning is more complicated than anticipated
    - How Hashing and StopWords works (NLTK/Hashlib)
    - Process behind Linear/Logistic Regression and modeling
    - Spark Vectors
    - NLP and ML as two separate theories and processes are hard work!
    - We were able to clean the dataset, analyze it, and break the phrases down in various ways so that they're ready to calculate by an ML model.
    - We were then able to assign values to phrases and have them output weights based on VADER Sentiment Analysis, but by presentation time were unable to feed a new phrase from our website into our Machine Learning model.
    - What our website accomplishes by our 10/25 presentation day is take input text, break it down into sentiment scores based on a Naive Bayes classifier and VADER Sentiment Analysis, and stores those phrases and their results into a SQLite database.
    - The input data collected in the SQLite database -- hopefully collected from friends, family and colleagues who test our text box -- can then be extracted, loaded, and transformed into a dataset usable as a training set in and of itself.
    - Basically, our presentation-ready application is at the least a data harvesting app.

    Our Vision

    Ultimately, we want to create a service that can scrape a user's input (online, in a game, on a forum, etc.) and determine how toxic that user's overall language is. This could be invaluable for mitigating the effect of internet trolling, cyber bullying, and toxic environments in general. In a world where so much interaction is virtual, it is important to discover ways to monitor our seemingly anonymous online behavior.
